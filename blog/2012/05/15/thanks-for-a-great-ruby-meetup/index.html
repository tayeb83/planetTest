Thanks for a Great Ruby Meetup

Last week we hosted the monthly Ruby Meetup at Cubox. We had a great level of talks and a good pressence of developers.

First Martin Loy from Guruhub gave a talk about scaling Rails. He compared the results at his company using different available Ruby webservers: Unicorn, Mizuno, Passenger and Torquebox. It was his first time working with Ruby and he shared his experiences and results with each server.

You can see his presentation on this link.

Then Federico Wagner from Artech talked about Genexus, a code generation tool, its Ruby generator and how Ruby is used in the company. It was an improvised talk and gave to very interesting discussion.

After the first two talks we had the pizzas, beer and sodas. As always, great discussions and conversations among the developers.

Last but not least, our own foca gave a talk about foreman, a command line application to manage procfile-based apps.

As always, thanks for coming to the Ruby meetup. If you’re interested in future events, check the meetup page. If you or your company wants to help out sponsoring a meetup, you’re more than welcome.

Ruby meetups will be held every second Tuesday of the month.
